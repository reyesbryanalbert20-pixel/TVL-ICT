The TVL-ICT

Information and Communication Technology Strand

What is TVL-ICT?

The TVL–ICT (Technical-Vocational-Livelihood – Information and Communications Technology) Strand is one of the programs offered under LCBA’s Senior High School TVL Track.

The TVL–ICT Strand is best suited for students who enjoy working with computers and technology, want to learn coding or web design, and are interested in pursuing careers in the digital and IT industry.

Main Goal of TVL-ICT

The strand aims to:

  • Equip students with technical computer skills
  • Provide real-world training in ICT-related fields
  • Prepare learners for employment, entrepreneurship, or college programs in IT

What Students Learn in TVL ICT?

Computer System Servicing

  • Assembling and maintaining computers
  • Installing operating systems and software
  • Troubleshooting hardware and software issues
  • Setting up basic computer networks
  • Following safety procedures and documenting service work

Programming and Coding

  • Basic coding skills
  • Introduction to software development
  • Creating simple programs and applications

Web Development

  • Designing websites using HTML and CSS
  • Adding interactivity through JavaScript
  • Building responsive web pages

Multimedia and Digital Design

  • Photo and video editing
  • Graphic design tools
  • Digital content creation

Advantages of Choosing TVL–ICT

Choosing this strand helps students:

  • Gain technical skills early
  • Earn National Certificates (NC II) from TESDA
  • Become job-ready after Senior High School
  • Start freelancing or tech-based businesses
  • Build a strong foundation for IT-related college courses

Possible TESDA Certifications

TVL–ICT students may qualify for:

  • Computer Systems Servicing NC II
  • Programming NC IV (depending on specialization)
  • Animation NC II
  • Contact Center Services NC II

These certificates are useful for employment locally and abroad.

Career Opportunities After TVL–ICT

  • Computer Technician
  • IT Support Staff
  • Web Developer
  • Network Installer
  • Graphic Designer
  • Programmer (entry-level)
  • Multimedia Editor
  • Freelance Digital Worker

College Courses Related to TVL–ICT

If students continue to college, good programs include:

Information Technology (IT)
Computer Science
Software Engineering
Multimedia Arts
Computer Engineering
